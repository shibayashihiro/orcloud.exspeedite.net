Carrier’s

 

Setting up Carriers begins with ensuring you have the carrier settings adjusted correctly, and this is easy to do. Go to the settings section, and you will see the list of carrier settings. The first thing is the financials, then terms and email notifications, making sure all parts are correct and up-to-date.

Once the settings are all correct, it is worth double checking all of them to be sure, you can then move on to the carrier profile screen.

Here you need to fill out all the information you have, and while you may not have enough to complete every part of the form, it is very important to ensure you have the email notification completed so the system can update things along the way.

Once all the available information is input there, especially the email notification, you then move on to creating a load. Once the load has been created, you can then assign a carrier in the text box. Typing in the first few letters of a carrier you have created in the system will allow you to select it, and then you can add on any dedicated trailers and so on the load requires if that is needed.

Once that is done, you can click on continue, which takes you to the trip/load screen. On the left you can click on the load itself, you can add the base, financials or fuel surcharge as applicable, it’s very quick to do from that screen, just input the numbers.

Also on the left, you can click the dropdown, which allows you to change the state of the load or send a freight agreement. Choose this and a new popup screen appears, this allows you to input the Base Amount, and add a note to the carrier, and then save the agreement. Doing this removes the load from the trip/load screen, and to see the load again, simply pick Carrier Freight Agreement Sent from the dropdown menu at the top. Here you will see all loads that have had a freight agreement sent to the carrier. Switching to email, we can find agreement confirmations, once an agreement is confirmed by the carrier, a load can then be changed to dispatch status using the dropdown on the left, which then moves the load back to the main status screen.

If a load needs changing at any point, you can, at any point, edit the load from this screen, but any changes of course require sending a new agreement for approval.

 

City Dispatch

 

Begin on the shipments screen, where we can sort or filter deliveries any way that suits the situation. You can search for specific recipients through the search box, or by clicking column headings, prioritize the list by that attribute.

Creating a load is straightforward, on the select shipment screen you will see a list of available shipments on the left, with the loads themselves on the right. You can create custom filters for shipment availability, for instance add a zip code, state or prefix and sort shipments by that. Simply go to the zone filter screen to set up appropriate filters.

You can simply drag and drop any shipment across to a load to add that shipment, and once you are happy, simply lick continue to get to the picks and drops page. You can change the way this is presented in settings, where it can be adjusted to show pick/drop/pick drop, or all picks together then all drops. The customizable interface is designed to work how you prefer, simply pick the one that suits. You can drag and drop each pick or drop until they are all in the right order.

Once everything is in order, simply click the green ‘Resources’ button, and you arrive at the resources screen. Here you can assign a driver, tractor and trailer as needed. If you use mobile communications, times of picks and drops can be assigned through the drop downs for each entry.

You can assign drops to different doors or to a single place depending on your needs, again, a dropdown on the left provides the relevant options. Once docked, the shipments are ready to be assigned to delivery loads as needed.

 

Client Rates

 

To add rates that can be assigned, go to the Client Rates List section, accessed through the Admin dropdown menu, it’s near the bottom. Here you simply clock on the ‘Add Rate’ green button, and here you can create any rate you wish, and name it for easy identification. The dropdown menu allows you to choose what category each rate is in, add the value and then add a description of each rate at the bottom.

To adjust client rates, you begin at the client screen. From here you can pick the client required, and simply select ‘add client rate’ from the dropdown. This takes you to the client rate input screen, where you can adjust all rates.

Firstly, you can easily apply a fuel surcharge, scroll to the bottom of the screen, where the fuel surcharge interface is on the right. Simply choose the appropriate fuel surcharge you wish to use then click apply to client, and it will automatically be added to the rates at the top of the screen.

Once you have input your rates, you can now apply any of these to your client in the client rates screen, simply add one or more rates in the client rates section at the bottom of the page, click apply and they will be added to the client rates for this client.

There are other options on the client rates screen, Pallet Rates allows you to set min and max charges pallets, and then apply that to a pallet number range through the ‘Show Pallet Rates’ button. You can add different charges for various ranges, such as 1-5 and then 5-10 or more simply by adding more rates through the ‘add pallet rate’ button.

Detention charges can be added in a similar way, with options for both Loaded and Unloaded detention rate as required.

Both Handling and Freight Charges operate on the same principal, adding charges and all required details places the charge on the client’s rate automatically.

 

Creating Shipments

 

Creating a shipment begins at the shipment page. The form itself is self-explanatory, simply requiring the relevant information to be added to each item.

For any shipment, clicking on the ‘Add Detail’ button allows you to input all relevant information, where you can input the commodity type, number of pallets, number of items, weight and any other special requirements such as temperature, hazmat etc. as needed. Once all relevant information is correct, simply save changes and they are applied to the new shipment. They will then automatically populate the details page of the shipment. You can add more commodities to any shipment as needed by clicking ‘add details’ again.

If you are using tracking software., you will see a tick next to each address associated with the shipment to confirm it is a validated address to aid efficiency. Once all details are correct, you can then simply click the green ‘Ready to Dispatch’ button to save the shipment.

In some cases, you may need to ship through several locations for a single client. Creating different address profiles under a single client can be achieved through the use of individual labels for each setting. For instance, setting a shipper with a different address to a recipient can accommodate different plants in the same organization, naming them appropriately makes it easy to track. This is useful for multiple drops to different branches of a single client, retaining all billing information in a single place while allowing you to treat each location separately for transport purposes.

Once shipments are delivered, you may still need to review them, shipment history is accessed through the shipment edit button of delivered shipments, and will allow you to see who picked and delivered the shipment, as well as full details of the load itself.

Driver Pay Setup

 

The first thing to note when looking to change driver pay settings is that it requires admin level users. Edit user through the admin menu takes you to the relevant screen, and under groups, Admin needs to be checked on to be able to make driver Pay changes.

Assuming that you have Admin access, then Driver Rates are accessed through the admin menu, and you begin by simply clicking the ‘Add Rates’ Button. The first item is a drop down that allows you to choose the category for the rate, then a zone the rate applies to, and then add a value for the rate itself. Click the green save button, and the rate is created and saved into the system.

The next step is to access the ‘Manage Rate Profile’ section, again, this is reached through the Admin menu. This applies to the different contracts you have, and here you can add rates, holiday pay, manual pay, range rates and so on as needed. Driver rates can be added to these contracts very simply by choosing the appropriate one from the list at the bottom and clicking ‘Apply Rate’. You can add more rates as required, and delete rates applying to the contract by clicking the delete symbol next to the rate at the top of the screen.

Adding a contract to a drive is done through the Drivers Screen, again, accessed through the Admin menu. Select a driver and click ‘Add Rate’, and then simply choose the right contract from the dropdown menu.

 

 

Driver Statements

 

Approving and saving driver settlements is an important part of the system of course, and we start out on the Drivers Screen, reached through the Profiles menu. Choose the driver you need on the list, and clicking the dropdown for that driver choose ‘View Bill History’ to proceed.

This will take you to the drivers own page, where you will be able to see all payments made to the driver previously. To begin, you first select the week you wish to work from using the dropdown on the left. This takes you to a new screen that includes all loads associated with the driver for that week, with a total of miles and so on all displayed.

You can review each load from here with all information provided, including pay. At this point you can add any Stop Off as required, and then save each load as you are happy with the information. Saved loads will now appear under the Settlement History for the driver, although note that the load is not finalized yet and can still be adjusted or even deleted from the driver’s history if needed. By clicking on the Dollar sign, you can go back and add new loads to the payment as required.

Once the list of payments is complete in the settlement history page for the week, you simply click on the ‘Update Driver Pay’ button, and this will take you to a summary page of all the loads, allowing you to recheck everything if needed. Once satisfied, you can then click the green ‘Finalize statement’ button at the bottom of the page. This will take you can to the driver’s settlement history page, but the entry will now show as ‘Finalized’ and you will be unable to edit or add to the week’s pay any further.

Once completed, you can then run a report and send out the settlement statement for each driver.

 

Driver Rates

 

To create your driver’s rates, begin at the Manage Drivers Rates screen, accessed through the Admin menu. Select the rate you wish to create, and you are taken to the Rate Profile screen. From here you have two buttons to add Manual Rates or a Range of Rates.

Adding Range of Rates presents you with a simple form, you can name the rate, then add the number of miles from and to that the range will apply, and finally the rate for this range of miles. Click submit to save the rate. You can add as many of these as you wish by simply repeating the process, clicking ‘Add’ then entering range and rate.

At the bottom of the screen is the ‘Select Driver Rates’ menu, and you can add new rates here as well. Click the green ‘Add Driver Rates’ button to the right to begin the new rates process. You will be presented with a new screen, this allows you to name the rate for easy reference later, and then choose a category for the rate via the dropdown menu. The next dropdown selects the zone the rate applies to if you wish, this is an optional part of the rate creation. Below you input the rate itself, and then you can select whether bonus applies to this rate or not, and finally you can add a description of the rate for future reference.

Once all information is entered, simply click the ‘Save Changes’ button to add the rates to the system. Once saved into the system, each Drivers Rate can be added to a Rate Contract in the Manage Drivers Rates screen as needed.

Once all driver rates, ranges and any manual rates are added to the contract, save the contract and return to the Driver Rates screen through the Admin menu. From here, each driver can be assigned to a contract through the dropdown menus next to each individual driver, and all the rates associated with that contract will be applied to that driver automatically.

 

FSC Setup

 

Setting up the Fuel Surcharge is done through the Manage Fuel Surcharge Schedule Screen, accessed via the admin menu. At the FSC Schedule screen, you are presented with the standard rates included in the database as well as the option to add your own rates through the green ‘Add FSC Schedule’ button.

If you have a CSV file of your present surcharges, you can click the browse button to search for the file and upload it to add your FSC schedule in one easy action. Once uploaded, selecting that schedule allows you to edit it as required, or even add new rates should they be needed. You can make changes to average price very quickly through the system, simply editing the average price at the top of any schedule screen, clicking update will update the entire schedule for you.

Once your schedule is set up correctly and adjusted for current average rates, applying it to any client is straightforward. Simply select the client from the client list and click ‘Add Client Rate’ button. On the add client rate screen, scroll to the bottom of the screen, where the fuel surcharge interface is on the right. Simply choose the appropriate fuel surcharge you wish to use then click apply to client, and it will automatically be added to the rates at the top of the screen.

 

Create a Load

 

Depending on how you operate, you reach the add load screen in two ways. If you are using a third-party system to populate the shipments in your system, you begin at the ‘Add Load’ button. If you are doing this manually, you will first have to create the relevant shipments through the Add Shipments screen.

Once we have our shipments in, the add load button shows us the shipments on the left, with the load open on the right. We can filter the shipments by client, destination, zone and so on as needed using the menu at the top to make finding the right shipment easier.

To add a shipment to a load, simply drag and drop the shipment into the load section on the right of the screen. Below the load a map will be shown, with a depiction of the route as a bird flies, that is straight lines between the points rather than a route.

Once the required shipments are added, click continue, which takes you to the next screen that allows you to sort the picks and drops for the load. You can drag and drop each pick or drop until they are in the right order, mileage is automatically calculated if you have the relevant software integrated. Once in the right order, clicking continue takes you to the Assign Resources Screen.

Here you can assign a driver, tractor, trailer or carrier as applicable for this load by simply choosing the appropriate option from the drop down menus. Once complete, saving the load takes you to the next screen, where you can dispatch the load from the drop down on the left.

OS&D

 

Managing OS&D is made simple by the system, from the OS&D report page, accessed through the Admin Menu, simply click ‘Add OS&D Report’ to begin the process. This presents you with an easy to use screen, allowing you to choose the type from a dropdown menu, the date, who is responsible for the damage, and a description of the incident.

From there, simply choose the shipment from the dropdown, and the system will populate the ‘From’ and ‘To’ parts of the screen along with the commodities. You then enter the number of pallets, any notes you need and an image is required.

Saving this takes you back to the main screen, and the report is filed.

 

Pallets

 

By going to the client list, through the Profiles menu, we see a list of clients as well as a list of all the pallets that each client has. You can adjust these by clicking through to the pallet adjustment field from here and simply changing the number and saving.

Alternatively, the Profiles menu also includes a direct link the Pallet Adjustment page, and here you can add or deduct pallets from any client, adding a comment as needed to track changes. Simply choose the client from the dropdown, the number of pallets involved and whether than are being added or deducted, set the date for the change and add any notes. Clicking save updates the client pallet list for you.

The system will automatically track pallets for you as the loads complete saving time.

 

Vacations

 

Adding vacations is very simple, from the Vacation Report Screen, click ‘Add Vacation’ then pick the driver from the dropdown menu, add in the start and end dates for the vacation, check whether its planned or approved, add any comments that you need. Then simply click save, and it is done.

 

 

 

Multi Company Multi Offices

 

Setting up Multi-Company or Multi-Offices begins at the settings screen reached through the Admin menu. Then simply click ’Multi’ to enter the Multi Menu. Setting Multi-Company to ‘True’ enables the Multi-Company features of the software.  

With Multi-Company enabled, the Admin menu will show you an Offices option below Companies, signaling the multi option is functioning. Going to the Office screen, we can click ‘Add Office’ which takes us to the next screen that allows us to input the required data. The first thing to do is assign this office to a company through the first drop down menu, you can add as many offices as needed to each company, and this ensures that all financial components of offices are assigned to the correct company.

From there you add in the required address, phone, email and so on, and then the Invoice Prefix. This is important as it is the code you use to differentiate this office from others within the company, create something that works for your system. You can do the same for invoice numbers and driver billing. Once complete just save the data and it will be added to the company data.

Once set up, assigning shipments to offices is simple, go to the Add Shipment screen, and select the Office required as you would a company from the dropdown menu. The relevant information is added automatically, then simply add in other details for the shipment as needed.

The Shipment system then works as normal, assigning to loads and drivers through the menu as before.

Under the Users Screen from the Admin Menu, you can set access to individual offices per user to enhance security. Simply select the User, and then pick which offices they will have access to by clicking the checkbox next to the chosen offices.
